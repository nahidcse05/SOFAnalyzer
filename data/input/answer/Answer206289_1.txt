<p>To view all available partitions you can try and use the lsblk command. </p>

<p>Open your terminal with <kbd>Ctrl</kbd> + <kbd> Alt</kbd> + <kbd> T</kbd> then type:</p>

<pre><code>sudo lsblk 
</code></pre>

<p>this will give you a list of all the drives / partitions available on the system.
Once you know the device you can mount it using the:</p>

<pre><code>sudo mount 
</code></pre>

<p>command.</p>
